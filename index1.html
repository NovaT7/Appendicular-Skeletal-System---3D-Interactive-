Upper Limb

The upper limb consists of 30 bones per arm, providing mobility and dexterity for manipulation and interaction with the environment.

Components:

  • Humerus - Upper arm bone
  • Radius - Lateral forearm bone
  • Ulna - Medial forearm bone
  • Carpals (8) - Wrist bones
  • Metacarpals (5) - Palm bones
  • Phalanges (14) - Finger bones

Key Functions:

  • Precision gripping and manipulation
  • Reaching and positioning
  • Support and weight bearing (limited)
  • Fine motor control

Lower Limb

The lower limb contains 30 bones per leg, designed for weight bearing, locomotion, and maintaining balance.

Components:

  • Femur - Thigh bone (longest bone)
  • Patella - Kneecap
  • Tibia - Shin bone
  • Fibula - Lateral leg bone
  • Tarsals (7) - Ankle bones
  • Metatarsals (5) - Foot bones
  • Phalanges (14) - Toe bones

Key Functions:

  • Support entire body weight
  • Locomotion (walking, running, jumping)
  • Balance and stability
  • Shock absorption

Girdles

The girdles connect the limbs to the axial skeleton, providing attachment points and facilitating movement.

Pectoral Girdle (Shoulder):

  • Clavicle - Collar bone
  • Scapula - Shoulder blade

Function: Provides mobility and range of motion for upper limbs

Pelvic Girdle (Hip):

  • Ilium - Upper hip bone
  • Ischium - Lower, posterior hip bone
  • Pubis - Anterior hip bone
  • Sacrum - Fused vertebrae (connection point)

Function: Provides stability and weight transfer from spine to lower limbs

Key Differences:

  • Pectoral: Lightweight, highly mobile
  • Pelvic: Strong, stable, weight-bearing
